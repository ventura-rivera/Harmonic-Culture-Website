We Are

Harmonic Culture

We are a kombucha brewery dedicated to sustainability. That's why we have decided to eliminate ALL bottling of our beverages. Instead, we offer our in-house creation: the Bev Station. You can fill up ANY of your reusable containers at our stations and be charged by the volume poured. This eliminates the energy input and environmental burden to prepare more glass, plastic, and other materials. Furthermore, it means we can charge customers less for a quality beverage! Help us build a new, harmonic culture that diverges from the standards of traditional beverage corporations.

Squad

Ventura graduated with a B.S. in Chemical and Biomolecular engineering from UC Berkeley in December 2017. Ever since, he has been finding time to work on the Harmonic Culture project while working as an IT business systems analyst at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. He has programmed, hardwired, and patented the Bev Station device and is working on finding a commercial kitchen for production. Ventura started this project to make a radical change in the beverage industry. He wants to eliminate the energy and environmental burden associated with producing glass and plastic bottles.

Ventura Rivera co-Founder/co-CEO

Erica is graduating from UC Berkeley in May 2018 with a B.S. in bioengineering. Erica has worked on other projects with Ventura; one of them was a student startup (AdipoCapsules) that won them 1st place of the Plant Based Meats 2.0 challenge sponsored by the Good Food Institute. They worked on finding plant alternatives to animal meat by incorporating plant fats into plant proteins that were then lypholyzed. The result was a powder that could be mechanically mixed into "plant-meat" and hold 3X the fat! Erica loves kombucha and all of its nutritional value. She works on the project to promote healthy habits and a harmonic culture between society and the environment.
